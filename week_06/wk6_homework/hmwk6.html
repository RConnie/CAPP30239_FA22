Mental Illness and Deadly Police Encounters

The following analysis uses police shooting data that looks at the number of individuals harmed by police who had a known mental illness. This analysis hopes to further the discussion around mental illness and safety, particularly to expand the narrative that mentally ill individuals have an increased risk of being harmed by police.

Mental Illness Deaths by State

This chart examines each state that reported police shootings in 2015 and looked at what percentage of those incidents involved a mentally ill person. This bar chart is skewed, as it does not give the entire picture of impact. Percentages could be high due to the fact that there were not many total shootings (ex: ME only had 4 incidents total, 2 of which were with mentally ill individuals). With an estimate of nearly 20% of Americans having a mental illness, this chart still shows an impact that disabled individuals may not receive the support they need if they are disproportionately involved in police encounters.

Mental Illness Deaths by Race

This series of ring charts divides police shootings by race and then looks at the number of shootings within each racial category that were of mentally ill individuals. It is interesting to look at intersectional identities to see if a pattern or outlier emerges. It would be interesting to investigate the incidents that had an unknown race since the total number of 146 shootings and had one of the biggest percentages of mentally ill individuals harmed.

If more time, I would fix the size of the rings and make the text more legible. I would also use a uniform color scheme so the rings match the other 2 charts.

Comparing Mental Illness Deaths by State

Texas, Florida, and California had the highest police shootings in 2015 and this multi-line graph compares the number of deaths they respectively had per month. The graph allows for the audience to see which month if any had peaks and can see if there are any patterns or outliers It would be insightful to have a multi-line graph of comparing each state against data from. another year to see how their police interactions with mentally ill individuals has changed.

If more time, I would fix the x-axis to make the months more legible.

All charts were created using 2015 data of police shootings was taken from the Washington Post Police Shootings Database
